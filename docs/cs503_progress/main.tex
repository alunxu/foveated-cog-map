\section{Question and headline finding}
% ==========================================================================

A PointNav agent (DD-PPO trained, Habitat sim) has two routes to the spatial information its policy needs: recompute it each step from the current visual encoder output, or maintain it across time inside the recurrent (LSTM) memory. These routes compete for finite hidden-state capacity. \emph{Does the structure of the visual input determine how this capacity gets allocated?} Our claim: a bottleneck at the encoder forces the LSTM to integrate the GPS sensor across time and store the result in a linearly-readable axis of $\mathbf{h}_2$ --- it has no other route; a rich encoder offers a viable per-step visual-feature route, and policy gradient reallocates capacity to it. The same hidden state encodes position in both regimes; only the \emph{format} differs.

% ==========================================================================
\section{Five conditions on one bandwidth axis}
% ==========================================================================

All agents share a $3$-layer LSTM ($512$-d) and the Wijmans non-visual sensor stack (goal-in-start-frame, GPS, compass, close-to-goal scalar); only the visual input changes. All four sighted conditions were retrained from scratch under unified hyperparameters ($\text{seed}{=}0$, $\text{num\_envs}{=}16$, $250$M frames), eliminating the inconsistency present in our \emph{Report 1} runs. All achieve $96$--$99\%$ success and SPL $\in [0.85, 0.89]$, so any cross-condition representational difference cannot be attributed to a skill gap.

For each condition we collect $500$ deterministic-policy episodes and run six analytical lenses on the top-layer hidden state $\mathbf{h}_2$: linear (Ridge $\alpha{=}10$) and MLP ($256$-d, $L_2{=}10^{-4}$) probes for GPS; lag-$k$ probe for $k{\in}\{0,1,2,5,10,20,50\}$; per-(unit, scene) Skaggs spatial information (rectified $\max(\mathbf{h}_2,0)$); cross-training probes at five intermediate checkpoints; and cross-condition CKA, Procrustes shape distance, and memory transplant. All probes use $5$-fold episode-level cross-validation. Single seed per condition follows the comparative-cognition modelling convention; rigour at the cross-condition level instead derives from convergence across the six lenses.

\section{Results: a Because-But-Therefore chain}
% ==========================================================================

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering\vspace{-4pt}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig/fig1_capacity_allocation.pdf}\vspace{-4pt}
\caption{Top-layer GPS $R^2$ ($5$-fold ep-CV, mean $\pm \sigma$) vs.\ encoder spatial output. Solid: linear (Ridge $\alpha{=}10$) probe. Dashed: $2$-layer MLP probe on the same hidden states. Blind point: preliminary $100$-ep partial NPZ.}\vspace{-4pt}
\label{fig:cap_alloc}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Because.} Linear GPS $R^2$ falls monotonically with encoder spatial output (Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc}, solid line) --- a robust trend across the four converged sighted conditions plus the independently-retrained blind. The monotonicity is what made foveated-logpolar (a $2{\times}2$-output rich-encoder control we added \emph{after} Report~1) a \emph{falsification target}: the encoder-bandwidth mechanism predicts it lands in the rich-encoder regime, and Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc} shows it does --- decisive evidence that the relevant axis is encoder bandwidth, not foveation specifically. The most direct alternative --- that the linear probe simply runs out of capacity in rich-encoder hidden states --- is ruled out by distance-to-goal, a same-dimensional control target which all five conditions decode at $R^2 > 0.8$.

\textbf{But.} A $2$-layer MLP fit on the \emph{same} hidden states recovers GPS in every condition (Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc}, dashed line), and the gap from linear widens with encoder bandwidth. Position information is not destroyed by a rich encoder; it gets pushed into non-linear $\mathbf{h}_2$ directions the linear DD-PPO action head cannot reach.

\textbf{Therefore.} The encoder and the recurrent state compete for representing position. A bottleneck (no encoder, or $1{\times}1$) leaves only one route: integrate the GPS sensor across time and store the result in a linearly-readable axis. A rich encoder opens a per-step visual-feature route; policy gradient reallocates capacity to it, and the integrated code stops being the linearly-readable axis --- though, as the MLP recovery shows, position remains encoded. \emph{Format differs; total information shifts only modestly.}

\section{Convergent evidence}
\label{sec:converg}
% ==========================================================================

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering\vspace{-4pt}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig/fig3_substitution_dynamics.pdf}\vspace{-4pt}
\caption{Linear GPS $R^2$ across training ($5$ checkpoints per condition; blind has only the $340$M ckpt and is omitted). Negative-$R^2$ outliers clipped to $-2.0$.}\vspace{-4pt}
\label{fig:subst}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Capacity allocation is a training-time process, not an endpoint property.} All four sighted conditions begin training with a high linear top-layer GPS code (Figure~\ref{fig:subst}); the linear axis is the LSTM's \emph{default} format. What separates conditions is what happens after: bottleneck preserves the integrated code; rich-encoder conditions decay as the visual route consolidates. This rules out the alternative that bottleneck conditions never learned the visual route --- they did, but policy gradient kept the integration route on top because there was no usable visual alternative.

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering\vspace{-4pt}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig/fig2_lagk_stability.pdf}\vspace{-4pt}
\caption{GPS $R^2$ ($5$-fold ep-CV) for past-position probes $\mathrm{GPS}_{t-k}$ from current $\mathbf{h}_t$. Blind point: preliminary $100$-ep partial NPZ.}\vspace{-4pt}
\label{fig:lagk}
\end{figure}

\textbf{The bottleneck-state code is integrated; the rich-encoder state is reactive.} Decoding past position $\mathrm{GPS}_{t-k}$ from current $\mathbf{h}_t$ (Figure~\ref{fig:lagk}) shows bottleneck conditions sustain stable positive $R^2$ across the lag range while rich-encoder ones degrade rapidly. The contrast is about format, not total information: same MLP recovery (Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc}), different temporal dynamics. Per-(unit, scene) Skaggs spatial information is condition-flat at $[1.16, 1.35]$ bits, within the within-condition spread --- ruling out the null reading that bottleneck conditions just have more place cells; the signal lives in the \emph{linear-readout axis}, not the per-unit metric.

\section{Deviations from the proposal, with explanations}
% ==========================================================================

\textbf{H1 reframed.} The proposal cast H1 as compensatory memory specifically in the foveated agent (eccentricity-stratified probe accuracy). \emph{Report 1}, on partially-converged checkpoints with heterogeneous hyperparameters, reported foveated as the longest-memory condition. Both the proposal framing and the Report~1 result are \emph{superseded}. After retraining all four sighted conditions under unified hyperparameters (the original cause of the apparent foveated specialness), and after adding a \emph{foveated-logpolar} control with intermediate ($2{\times}2$) encoder spatial output as a falsification test, the data show a monotonic encoder-bandwidth--vs--linear-$R^2$ trend (Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc}) under which foveated and uniform sit in the same rich-encoder regime. The mechanism is encoder bandwidth, not foveation specifically.

\textbf{H3 de-scoped, methodology broadened.} The proposal's H3 (epistemic gaze: learned-gaze MLP targeting memory-weak regions) was de-scoped after the gaze decoder collapsed to a fixed point under pure navigation reward; a bounded-$\sigma$ stochastic-gaze fix is in the codebase but not pursued this iteration. Compensating, we imported a richer set of cognitive-neuroscience methods into the representational-analysis frame: Skaggs spatial information as a place-cell signature on $\mathbf{h}_2$, an SR-style predictive-horizon probe, Procrustes shape-metric and principal-angle subspace divergence, an excursion-forgetting protocol, and a leave-one-scene-out scene-invariance test. The contribution shifts from a single epistemic-gaze deliverable to recasting classical cognitive-neuroscience metrics as a computational framework for representational analysis of deep-RL agents.

\section{Problems encountered, solutions, and forward risks}
% ==========================================================================

\textbf{Encountered \& solved.} (i)~\emph{Hyperparameter drift across Report~1 conditions} (different $\text{num\_envs}$, $\text{lr}$, frame budgets) confounded cross-condition comparisons. Resolved by retraining all four sighted conditions from scratch under a unified config. (ii)~\emph{Skaggs spatial information $\sim 100\times$ inflated} relative to the place-cell literature when computed on raw $\mathbf{h}_2$, because near-zero global means dominate the ratio in $I = \sum p(b)(\lambda_b/\bar\lambda)\log_2(\lambda_b/\bar\lambda)$. Resolved by applying ReLU rectification ($\max(\mathbf{h}_2, 0)$) before computing per-bin firing rates, recovering paper-magnitude bits. (iii)~\emph{Cross-condition memory transplant on coarse} failed at the state-dict load (incompatible RGB shape: $48{\times}48$ vs $256{\times}256$). Resolved by restricting to the $6$ sighted-only pairs sharing $256{\times}256$ input; coarse-and-blind transplants pending an architecture-side fix. (iv)~\emph{Blind probe-collect at $50$ episodes gave catastrophically wide CV folds} (linear $-0.27\pm 1.41$). Diagnosed as sample-size noise rather than a methodological bug: the same probe at $100$ episodes stabilises to $+0.79\pm 0.25$.

\textbf{Likely to encounter.} (a)~\emph{Blind $500$-ep collection ETA} ($\sim 6$h on RCP) overlaps with the parallel NeurIPS submission deadline; the $100$-ep partial NPZ provides a fall-back. (b)~\emph{Single-seed per condition} bounds the seed-variance error bars; multi-seed re-runs across all five conditions are out of compute scope for this iteration. (c)~\emph{Transplant matrix coverage} is incomplete; without coarse-and-blind pairs we cannot test the bottleneck-vs-rich-encoder asymmetry directly.
