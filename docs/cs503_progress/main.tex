\section{Question and headline finding}
% ==========================================================================

\textbf{Question.} A PointNav agent~\cite{wijmans2020ddppo,savva2019habitat} has two routes to the spatial information its policy needs to act: recompute it each step from the current visual encoder output, or maintain it across time inside the recurrent (LSTM) memory. These routes compete for finite hidden-state capacity. \emph{Does the structure of the visual input determine how this capacity gets allocated?}

\textbf{Theoretical claim.} Encoder bandwidth and recurrent integration are competing routes for representing position. A bottleneck at the encoder forces the LSTM to integrate the GPS sensor across time and store the result in a linearly-readable axis of $\mathbf{h}_2$ --- it has no other route. A rich encoder offers a viable per-step visual-feature route; policy gradient reallocates capacity to it, and the linearly-readable component of $\mathbf{h}_2$ collapses. \emph{The same hidden state encodes position in both regimes; only the format differs.}

\section{Five conditions on one bandwidth axis}
% ==========================================================================

All agents share a $3$-layer LSTM ($512$-d) and the Wijmans~\cite{wijmans2023emergence} non-visual sensor stack (goal-in-start-frame, GPS, compass, close-to-goal scalar); only the visual input changes. All four sighted conditions were retrained from scratch under unified hyperparameters ($\text{seed}{=}0$, $\text{num\_envs}{=}16$, $250$M frames), eliminating the inconsistency present in our \emph{Report 1} runs. All achieve $96$--$99\%$ success and SPL $\in [0.85, 0.89]$, so any cross-condition representational difference cannot be attributed to a skill gap.

For each condition we collect $500$ deterministic-policy episodes and run six analytical lenses on the top-layer hidden state $\mathbf{h}_2$: linear (Ridge $\alpha{=}10$) and MLP ($256$-d, $L_2{=}10^{-4}$) probes for GPS; lag-$k$ probe for $k{\in}\{0,1,2,5,10,20,50\}$; per-(unit, scene) Skaggs spatial information (rectified $\max(\mathbf{h}_2,0)$); cross-training probes at five intermediate checkpoints; and cross-condition CKA, Procrustes shape distance, and memory transplant. All probes use $5$-fold episode-level cross-validation. Single seed per condition follows the comparative-cognition modelling convention; rigour at the cross-condition level instead derives from convergence across the six lenses.

\section{Results: a Because-But-Therefore chain}
% ==========================================================================

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering\vspace{-4pt}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig/fig1_capacity_allocation.pdf}\vspace{-4pt}
\caption{Top-layer GPS $R^2$ ($5$-fold ep-CV, mean $\pm \sigma$) vs.\ encoder spatial output. Solid: linear (Ridge $\alpha{=}10$) probe. Dashed: $2$-layer MLP probe on the same hidden states. Blind point: preliminary $100$-ep partial NPZ.}\vspace{-4pt}
\label{fig:cap_alloc}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Because.} Linear GPS $R^2$ falls monotonically with encoder spatial output (Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc}, solid line) --- a robust trend across the four converged sighted conditions plus the independently-retrained blind. The monotonicity is what made foveated-logpolar (a $2{\times}2$-output rich-encoder control we added \emph{after} Report~1) a \emph{falsification target}: the encoder-bandwidth mechanism predicts it lands in the rich-encoder regime, and Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc} shows it does --- decisive evidence that the relevant axis is encoder bandwidth, not foveation specifically. The most direct alternative --- that the linear probe simply runs out of capacity in rich-encoder hidden states --- is ruled out by distance-to-goal, a same-dimensional control target which all five conditions decode at $R^2 > 0.8$.

\textbf{But.} A $2$-layer MLP fit on the \emph{same} hidden states recovers GPS in every condition (Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc}, dashed line), and the gap from linear widens with encoder bandwidth. Position information is not destroyed by a rich encoder; it gets pushed into non-linear $\mathbf{h}_2$ directions the linear DD-PPO action head cannot reach.

\textbf{Therefore.} The encoder and the recurrent state compete for representing position. A bottleneck (no encoder, or $1{\times}1$) leaves only one route: integrate the GPS sensor across time and store the result in a linearly-readable axis. A rich encoder opens a per-step visual-feature route; policy gradient reallocates capacity to it, and the integrated code stops being the linearly-readable axis --- though, as the MLP recovery shows, position remains encoded. \emph{Format differs; total information shifts only modestly.}

\section{Convergent evidence}
\label{sec:converg}
% ==========================================================================

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering\vspace{-4pt}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig/fig3_substitution_dynamics.pdf}\vspace{-4pt}
\caption{Linear GPS $R^2$ across training ($5$ checkpoints per condition; blind has only the $340$M ckpt and is omitted). Negative-$R^2$ outliers clipped to $-2.0$.}\vspace{-4pt}
\label{fig:subst}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Capacity allocation is a training-time process, not an endpoint property.} Re-probing intermediate checkpoints (Figure~\ref{fig:subst}) shows all four sighted conditions begin training with a high linear top-layer GPS code; the linear axis is the \emph{default} format the LSTM acquires. What separates conditions is what happens \emph{after}: bottleneck (coarse) preserves the integrated code through training; rich-encoder conditions decay as the visual route consolidates. Decay rate tracks encoder informativeness about position. This rules out the alternative that bottleneck conditions never \emph{learned} the visual route --- they learned it, then policy gradient kept the integration route on top because there was no usable visual alternative.

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering\vspace{-4pt}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig/fig2_lagk_stability.pdf}\vspace{-4pt}
\caption{GPS $R^2$ ($5$-fold ep-CV) for past-position probes $\mathrm{GPS}_{t-k}$ from current $\mathbf{h}_t$. Blind point: preliminary $100$-ep partial NPZ.}\vspace{-4pt}
\label{fig:lagk}
\end{figure}

\textbf{The bottleneck-state code is integrated; the rich-encoder state is reactive.} Decoding past position from current $\mathbf{h}_t$ (Figure~\ref{fig:lagk}) shows bottleneck conditions sustain stable positive $R^2$ across the lag range, while rich-encoder ones degrade rapidly. A smoothly-varying \emph{integrated} code preserves past-position information; a \emph{reactive} code keyed to current visual context does not. The contrast is about format: same total information (per the MLP recovery in Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc}), but different temporal dynamics.

\textbf{Place-cell signature is condition-flat.} Per-(unit, scene) Skaggs spatial information lies in $[1.16, 1.35]$ bits across all five conditions, well within the within-condition spread. The bandwidth-allocation signal lives in the \emph{linear-readout axis}, not in the per-unit place-cell metric --- defending against the obvious null reading that bottleneck conditions simply have more place cells.

\section{What changed since Report 1; status; risks}
% ==========================================================================

\textbf{Reframe of H1.} \emph{Report 1} reported foveated as having the longest temporal memory (lag-$5$ $R^2{=}0.629$, the only condition still positive). This is \emph{overturned}: under unified retraining and converged probes, foveated's lag-$k$ profile is \emph{less} stable than coarse's (Figure~\ref{fig:lagk}). The new mechanism (encoder bandwidth) makes the same predictions for blind/coarse/uniform/foveated and additionally predicts that foveated-logpolar (a $2{\times}2$ rich-encoder control not in Report~1) lands in the rich-encoder regime --- which it does (Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc}). The Report~1 ranking reflected partially-converged training plus heterogeneous hyperparameters across conditions; the post-retrain analysis above is the corrected picture.

\textbf{H3 de-scoped, methodology direction broadened.} The proposal's H3 (epistemic gaze: learned-gaze MLP targeting memory-weak regions) was de-scoped after the gaze decoder collapsed to a fixed point under pure navigation reward (a bounded-$\sigma$ stochastic-gaze fix is in the codebase but not pursued this iteration). Compensating, we imported a richer set of cognitive-neuroscience methods into the representational-analysis frame: Skaggs spatial information as a place-cell signature on $\mathbf{h}_2$, an SR-style predictive-horizon probe, Procrustes shape-metric and principal-angle subspace divergence, and an excursion-forgetting protocol. The contribution shifts from a single epistemic-gaze deliverable to recasting classical cognitive-neuroscience metrics as a computational framework for representational analysis of deep-RL agents.

\textbf{Status \& risks.} Phase~A probe-collect $4/5$ complete; blind $500$-ep run in flight on EPFL RCP ($\sim 6$h ETA, $100$-ep partial NPZ already used in Figures~\ref{fig:cap_alloc} and~\ref{fig:lagk}). Phase~B complete for the four sighted conditions; blind row queued. The $50$-ep blind preview was catastrophically wide ($-0.27\pm 1.41$); $100$ eps stabilised to $+0.79\pm 0.25$; full $500$ eps should tighten std to $\sim 0.10$.
