\section{Project Rescope and Current Direction}
% ==========================================================================

The \href{https://raw.githubusercontent.com/alunxu/cs503-proposal/main/How_Foveated_Vision_Shapes_Cognitive_Maps_in_Navigation_Agents.pdf}{proposal} framed the question around foveated vision and proposed a learned-gaze experiment (H3). Two deviations organise this report. \textbf{H1 reframed:} training the four sighted conditions under unified hyperparameters showed no foveated-specific compensatory effect, and a foveated-logpolar control landed in the rich-encoder regime as predicted by the encoder-bandwidth account; the substantive variable appears to be encoder bandwidth, not foveation. \textbf{H3 de-scoped:} the learned-gaze MLP collapsed under navigation reward, and we redirected effort to importing classical cognitive-neuroscience methods (Skaggs spatial information, SR-style predictive-horizon, Procrustes shape-metric, subspace divergence, excursion-forgetting) into the representational-analysis frame. The revised question: does visual-input structure shape the \emph{format} of the cognitive map an agent builds in recurrent memory?

\noindent\textbf{Capacity-allocation hypothesis}\quad A PointGoal navigation (PointNav) agent has two routes for spatial information: (i) recompute from the visual encoder each step, or (ii) integrate the GPS sensor across time in the LSTM memory. These routes compete for finite hidden-state capacity. A bottleneck leaves only the integration route, so the LSTM carries a temporally-stable, linearly-readable position code in the policy-readable layer ($\mathbf{h}_2$). A rich encoder opens the visual route; capacity flows toward it and the linear component of $\mathbf{h}_2$ shrinks while position stays recoverable non-linearly. Same task, different cognitive-map format~\cite{banino2018grid,cueva2018grid}. The hypothesis makes three falsifiable predictions tested below: linear $\mathbf{h}_2$ GPS $R^2$ should fall with encoder bandwidth; a $2$-layer MLP should still recover GPS; and lag-$k$ stability should diverge between bottleneck and rich-encoder regimes.

\section{Setup and method}
% ==========================================================================

We hold architecture, training, and the non-visual sensor stack constant across five conditions and vary only the visual input (Table~\ref{tab:conditions}). All agents share a $3$-layer LSTM ($512$-d) and the goal-in-start-frame / GPS / compass / close-to-goal sensor stack of~\cite{wijmans2023emergence}. The four sighted conditions are trained from scratch under unified hyperparameters ($250$M frames); blind uses a $340$M checkpoint, since blind agents need longer training to converge~\cite{tolman1948cognitive,wijmans2023emergence}. All achieve $96$--$99\%$ success and SPL $\in [0.85, 0.89]$ --- no skill gap.

\textbf{Condition} & \textbf{Spatial bandwidth} & \textbf{Visual input}\\
\midrule

\end{tabular}\vspace{-2pt}

The conditions span the encoder spatial-bandwidth axis: \emph{blind} is the no-vision lower bound~\cite{wijmans2023emergence}; \emph{coarse} retains visual input but compresses it through a $1{\times}1$ encoder; \emph{foveated-logpolar} is an intermediate-bandwidth ($2{\times}2$) falsification target; \emph{foveated} and \emph{uniform} are rich-encoder endpoints (foveated has peripheral blur).

For each condition we collect $500$ deterministic-policy episodes and analyse the frozen top-layer hidden state $\mathbf{h}_2$ along three probing families~\cite{belinkov2022probing}. \textbf{Decodability} (\emph{what does $\mathbf{h}_2$ encode?}) --- linear (Ridge $\alpha{=}10$, what a linear policy head can read) and $2$-layer MLP probes for GPS, lag-$k$ probes for past position $\mathrm{GPS}_{t-k}$, per-(unit, scene) Skaggs spatial information, and cross-training probes at $5$ checkpoints. \textbf{Intrinsic structure} (\emph{do conditions share a code?}) --- linear CKA~\cite{kornblith2019cka}, with Procrustes shape-distance in progress. \textbf{Causal intervention} (\emph{does the policy rely on $\mathbf{h}_2$?}) --- cross-condition memory transplant (sighted-only; coarse / blind pairs deferred). All probes use $5$-fold episode-level cross-validation. This report focuses on decodability; the other two families are still in progress (§\ref{sec:open}).

\section{Findings}
% ==========================================================================

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering\vspace{-4pt}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig/fig1_capacity_allocation.pdf}\vspace{-4pt}
\caption{Linear (filled) and $2$-layer MLP (open) probes for GPS, per condition. Linear $R^2$ falls monotonically with encoder spatial bandwidth, while the MLP probe recovers GPS in every condition: position information is retained in non-linear $\mathbf{h}_2$ directions a linear policy head cannot read.}\vspace{-4pt}
\label{fig:cap_alloc}
\end{figure}

\noindent\textbf{Encoder bandwidth shapes the linear axis}\quad Linear GPS $R^2$ falls smoothly as encoder spatial bandwidth grows (Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc}, solid). The trend also serves as falsification: foveated-logpolar (a $2{\times}2$ rich-encoder control) was predicted to land in the rich-encoder regime, and the data are consistent with that prediction --- arguing against the alternative that foveation specifically shapes the cognitive map. A second alternative --- that the linear probe simply lacks capacity in rich-encoder states --- is ruled out by a same-dimensional distance-to-goal control which decodes at $R^2 > 0.8$ in every condition. A $2$-layer MLP probe on the same hidden states recovers GPS everywhere (Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc}, dashed), and the linear--MLP gap widens with encoder bandwidth: position information is retained, just not as the linearly-readable axis. The bottleneck leaves only the integration route, so position lands on a linearly-readable axis; a rich encoder opens a visual route and the integrated code stops being linearly readable, yet stays non-linearly recoverable. Format differs at convergence.

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering\vspace{-4pt}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig/fig3_substitution_dynamics.pdf}\vspace{-4pt}
\caption{Linear GPS $R^2$ across training. All four sighted conditions emerge with a high linear top-layer GPS code at $\sim 50$M frames; bottleneck (coarse) preserves the integrated code through training, while the three rich-encoder conditions decay as the visual route consolidates --- decay rate ordered by encoder informativeness about position. Blind: $340$M endpoint only (real point); the dashed line is the predicted trajectory under the capacity-allocation account, pending re-probing of intermediate blind checkpoints (§\ref{sec:open}, A3).}\vspace{-4pt}
\label{fig:subst}
\end{figure}

\noindent\textbf{Allocation emerges across training}\quad But did bottleneck conditions ever \emph{learn} the visual route? Re-probing at intermediate checkpoints (Figure~\ref{fig:subst}) suggests they did: all four sighted conditions begin with a high linear top-layer GPS code; the linear axis is the LSTM's \emph{default} early format. What separates conditions is what happens \emph{after}: bottleneck preserves the integrated code; the three rich-encoder conditions decay as the visual route consolidates, with decay rate ordered by encoder informativeness about position. Capacity allocation thus appears to be a training-time process, not an endpoint property --- bottleneck conditions kept the integration route on top because no usable visual alternative emerged.

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering\vspace{-4pt}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig/fig2_lagk_stability.pdf}\vspace{-4pt}
\caption{Past-target decoding from current $\mathbf{h}_t$ across lag $k$, for two spatial targets: GPS (left) and compass (right). Among the sighted conditions the bottleneck-vs-rich-encoder format contrast extends beyond GPS to compass --- coarse sustains stable positive $R^2$ on both, while uniform degrades on both. Blind's GPS profile fits the bottleneck pattern; its compass row is provisional under the in-progress probe-collect (§\ref{sec:open}, A1) and not yet stable. (Negative-$R^2$ outliers clipped at $-2.0$.)}\vspace{-4pt}
\label{fig:lagk}
\end{figure}

\noindent\textbf{Bottleneck integrates; rich encoder reacts}\quad The format difference also shows up within an episode and across two spatial targets. Decoding past GPS or compass from $\mathbf{h}_t$ (Figure~\ref{fig:lagk}) shows bottleneck conditions sustaining stable positive $R^2$ across lags while rich-encoder conditions degrade: an \emph{integrated} code preserves past spatial information; a \emph{reactive} code keyed to current visual context does not. The contrast generalises across both targets --- it appears to be about format, not GPS specifically. (Blind's compass row is provisional pending the in-progress probe-collect.) As a check against the obvious null that bottleneck conditions just have more place cells, per-(unit, scene) Skaggs spatial information~\cite{wijmans2023emergence} is condition-flat at $[1.16, 1.35]$ bits --- the bandwidth-allocation signal seems to live in linear readout rather than per-unit place-cell metric. Together the three findings suggest encoder bandwidth shapes the \emph{format}, not the existence, of the cognitive map.

\section{Open issues and action items}
\label{sec:open}
% ==========================================================================

\textbf{Forward risks}\quad Two scientific risks remain. The bandwidth-allocation account makes falsifiable predictions for several of the planned causal-intervention and scope-generalisation tests below; negative outcomes there would force a more nuanced reading of the encoder--memory race. Single-seed per condition also leaves seed-variance unbounded --- the cross-condition rank ordering should survive but absolute magnitudes are seed-exposed.

\textbf{Bandwidth-allocation refinements}\quad (A1) complete blind probe-collect and re-probe intermediate blind checkpoints, populating the blind row across all lenses (completes Figs~\ref{fig:subst}--\ref{fig:lagk}); (A2) retrain coarse with $256{\times}256$-padded input so the transplant matrix can include coarse pairs (closes the H2 evidence gap); (A3) two-seed validation on coarse and uniform endpoints to bound seed-variance.

\textbf{New cog-neuro analyses on existing $\mathbf{h}_2$}\quad (A4) allocentric occupancy decoder $f(\mathbf{h}_T,\mathbf{c}_T) \to 32{\times}32$ binary map --- tests whether memory carries a metric \emph{map} rather than just a position scalar (ground-truth ready); (A5) MINE-based predictive-horizon $I(\mathbf{h}_t; \text{pos}_{t+k})$ for $k \in \{1,5,10,20\}$; (A6) successor-representation eigenbasis test linking $\mathbf{h}_2$ PCs to the eigenvectors of the policy's successor matrix; (A7) Sussillo--Barak fixed-point analysis on $\mathbf{h}_2$; (A8) place-vs-view-cell dissociation, boundary-vector tuning, ratemap drift, and hierarchical scene-clustering as a relational map test.

\textbf{Scope-generalisation}\quad (A9) foveation strength sweep ($\sigma_{\max} \in \{2,4,12,20\}$) as a continuous lever on the encoder--memory race; (A10) transformer navigator under the same five conditions, probing attention / KV-cache for position --- an architecture-agnostic falsification target; H3 reattempt with bounded-$\sigma$ stochastic gaze if time permits.

\section{Implications}
% ==========================================================================

Three implications follow. (i) Encoder bandwidth becomes a \emph{design lever} for recurrent memory: restricting encoder output may recruit integration-style memory an unrestricted pipeline crowds out~\cite{pourrahimi2025foveated}. (ii) The encoder--memory race is architecture-agnostic: VLMs on rich foundation encoders may exhibit similar crowd-out, contributing to the spatial-reasoning gap~\cite{ramakrishnan2025space}. (iii) The lens ports cognitive-neuroscience methodology to deep-RL.
