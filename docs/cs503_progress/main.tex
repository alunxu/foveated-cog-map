\section{Question and headline finding}
% ==========================================================================

\textbf{Question.} A PointNav agent~\cite{wijmans2020ddppo,savva2019habitat} has two routes to spatial information its policy needs to act: recompute it each step from the current visual encoder output, or maintain it across time inside the recurrent (LSTM) memory. These routes compete for finite hidden-state capacity. \emph{Does the structure of the visual input determine how this capacity gets allocated?}

\textbf{Headline finding.} Across five conditions that share architecture, sensor stack, training budget, and within-episode behaviour ($96\!\!-\!\!99\%$ success), the linear top-layer GPS $R^2$ falls \emph{monotonically} with encoder spatial output dimension (Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc}, solid line). The same hidden states recover GPS at $R^2 \in [0.46, 0.92]$ under a 2-layer MLP probe (Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc}, dashed line) --- so the contrast is about \emph{linear} top-layer readability (the operation the DD-PPO action head performs), not information loss.

\textbf{Theoretical claim.} Bottleneck conditions force the LSTM to integrate the GPS sensor across time and store the result in a linearly-readable axis of $\mathbf{h}_2$ --- there is no other route. A rich encoder offers a viable per-step visual-feature route; policy gradient reallocates capacity to it, and the integrated code is no longer the linearly-readable axis. The same hidden state encodes position in both regimes; only the format differs.

\section{Five conditions on one bandwidth axis}
% ==========================================================================

All agents share a $3$-layer LSTM ($512$-d) and the Wijmans~\cite{wijmans2023emergence} non-visual sensor stack (goal-in-start-frame, GPS, compass, close-to-goal scalar); only the visual input changes. All four sighted conditions were retrained from scratch under unified hyperparameters ($\text{seed}{=}0$, $\text{num\_envs}{=}16$, $250$M frames), eliminating the hyperparameter inconsistency present in our \emph{Report 1} runs. All achieve $96$--$99\%$ success and SPL $\in [0.85, 0.89]$, so any cross-condition representational difference cannot be attributed to a skill gap.

For each condition we collect $500$ deterministic-policy episodes ($\sim 30$k--$120$k LSTM steps) and run six analytical lenses on the top-layer $\mathbf{h}_2$: linear (Ridge $\alpha{=}10$) and MLP ($256$-d, $L_2{=}10^{-4}$) probes for GPS; lag-$k$ probe for $k\in\{0,1,2,5,10,20,50\}$; per-(unit, scene) Skaggs spatial information (rectified $\max(\mathbf{h}_2,0)$); cross-training probes at five intermediate checkpoints; and cross-condition CKA, Procrustes shape distance, and memory transplant. All probes use $5$-fold episode-level cross-validation. Single seed per condition follows the comparative-cognition modelling convention; rigour at the cross-condition level instead derives from convergence across the six lenses.

\section{Results: a Because-But-Therefore chain}
% ==========================================================================

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering\vspace{-4pt}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig/fig1_capacity_allocation.pdf}\vspace{-4pt}
\caption{\textbf{The capacity-allocation principle, magnitude axis.} Top-layer GPS $R^2$ ($5$-fold ep-CV mean $\pm \sigma$) plotted against encoder spatial output. Solid line / filled markers: linear (Ridge) probe --- monotonic anti-correlation with encoder bandwidth, dropping from $+0.79$ (blind) to $-1.19$ (uniform). Dashed line / open markers: $2$-layer MLP probe on the \emph{same} hidden states --- recovery in every condition, gap widens with bandwidth. Position information is not destroyed by a rich encoder; it gets pushed into non-linear $\mathbf{h}_2$ directions a linear policy head cannot read. Blind row from preliminary $100$-ep partial NPZ; full $500$-ep collection in flight.}\vspace{-4pt}
\label{fig:cap_alloc}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Because.} Linear GPS $R^2$ falls monotonically with encoder spatial output (Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc}, solid line). Bottleneck conditions retain the linear code ($+0.79$ blind, $+0.58$ coarse); rich-encoder conditions lose it ($+0.16$ foveated, $-1.19$ uniform). Foveated-logpolar with a $2{\times}2$ encoder sits at $-0.12$, which is the H1-mechanism prediction made \emph{after} Report 1: a third rich-encoder condition with intermediate spatial output should land in the rich-encoder regime, and it does. Distance-to-goal --- a same-dimensional control target --- decodes at $R^2 \in [0.81, 0.96]$ in every condition, ruling out a probe-capacity-collapse reading.

\textbf{But.} A $2$-layer MLP fit on the \emph{same} hidden states recovers GPS in every condition (Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc}, dashed line). The MLP-minus-linear gap widens monotonically with encoder bandwidth ($+0.13$ blind, $+0.22$ coarse, $+0.59$ fov-logpolar, $+0.60$ foveated, $+1.65$ uniform). Position information is not destroyed by a rich encoder; it gets pushed into non-linear $\mathbf{h}_2$ directions that the linear DD-PPO action head cannot read.

\textbf{Therefore.} The most parsimonious account is the capacity-allocation principle stated above: \emph{integration route} (carry sensor across time) and \emph{visual-feature route} (substitute encoder features for the integrated quantity) compete for finite recurrent capacity, and encoder bandwidth is the knob that determines which route wins. The substitution can be \emph{policy-relevant} or \emph{format-only}; Section~\ref{sec:converg} disentangles these.

\section{Convergent evidence}
\label{sec:converg}
% ==========================================================================

Three additional lenses corroborate the capacity-allocation account, each addressing an alternative explanation.

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering\vspace{-4pt}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig/fig2_lagk_stability.pdf}\vspace{-4pt}
\caption{\textbf{Lag-$k$ temporal stability separates integrated from reactive memory.} Decoding the past position $\mathrm{GPS}_{t-k}$ from the current $\mathbf{h}_t$. Bottleneck conditions (blind, coarse) sustain stable positive $R^2$ across the lag range; rich-encoder conditions (foveated, fov-logpolar, uniform) degrade rapidly. Bottleneck $\mathbf{h}_t$ is a smoothly-varying integrated code; rich-encoder $\mathbf{h}_t$ is reactive to current visual context. Blind$^\dagger$ row from preliminary $100$-ep partial NPZ.}\vspace{-4pt}
\label{fig:lagk}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Substitution dynamics across training.} Re-probing intermediate checkpoints ($\approx 50/100/150/200/250$M frames) shows that \emph{all four sighted conditions start at high linear GPS $R^2$ at $\sim 50$M frames} (coarse $+0.91$, foveated $+0.91$, uniform $+0.83$, foveated-logpolar $+0.92$); the linear top-layer code emerges early across the bandwidth spectrum. Bottleneck (coarse) preserves $R^2$ around $+0.5$ through training; rich-encoder conditions decay --- uniform crosses zero by $\sim 200$M and lands at $-1.79$, foveated drifts to $+0.18$, foveated-logpolar to $-0.03$. Decay rate tracks encoder informativeness about position --- this is capacity allocation as a \emph{training-time process}, not a property of the converged endpoint.

\textbf{Lag-$k$ temporal stability.} Figure~\ref{fig:lagk}: bottleneck conditions sustain stable positive $R^2$ across $k\in\{0,2,5,10,20\}$; rich-encoder conditions are unstable. The \emph{format} of the bottleneck-state code is a smoothly-varying integrated quantity; the rich-encoder state is reactive to the current visual scene and does not extrapolate temporally.

\textbf{Place-cell signature is condition-flat.} Per-(unit, scene) Skaggs spatial information is essentially condition-flat: $1.20$ (coarse), $1.24$ (foveated), $1.19$ (uniform), $1.16$ (foveated-logpolar); blind $1.35$ (preliminary). $\Delta \approx 0.15$ bits, well within the within-condition spread. The bandwidth-allocation signal lives in the \emph{linear-readout axis}, not in the per-unit place-cell metric. This is a defence against the obvious null reading (``maybe blind/coarse just have more place cells''): they do not.

\section{What changed since Report 1; status; risks}
% ==========================================================================

\textbf{Reframe.} \emph{Report 1} reported foveated as having the longest temporal memory ($R^2{=}0.629$ at lag-$5$, the only condition still positive). This is \emph{overturned}. The Report 1 ranking reflected partially-converged training plus heterogeneous hyperparameters across conditions; after unified retraining, foveated's lag-$k$ is \emph{less} stable than coarse's, not more (Figure~\ref{fig:lagk}). The new mechanism (encoder bandwidth) makes the same predictions for blind/coarse/uniform/foveated and additionally predicts that foveated-logpolar (a $2{\times}2$ rich-encoder control not in Report 1) lands in the rich-encoder regime --- which it does ($-0.12$, Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc}).

\textbf{Status.} Phase~A probe-collect: $4/5$ complete; blind $500$-ep run in flight on EPFL RCP ($\sim 6$h ETA, partial $100$-ep NPZ already used in both figures above). Phase~B (linear/MLP/lag-$k$/Skaggs/substitution/CKA-Procrustes-transplant) complete for the four sighted conditions; the blind row of each is queued to fire automatically once the $500$-ep NPZ lands. Pre-DDL queue: re-run lenses with converged blind, apply values to the NeurIPS submission, sanity-rebuild the paper PDF.

\textbf{Risks tracked.} (a)~The $50$-ep blind preview gave catastrophically wide CV folds (linear $-0.27\pm 1.41$); at $100$ eps the same probe stabilised at $+0.79\pm 0.25$ (the value plotted). We expect $500$ eps to tighten std to $\sim 0.10$. (b)~A separately-trained blind on a different seed/effective-batch gives a different mean ($+0.36$); we use our own retrained checkpoint and treat the alternative as one cross-setup robustness check.
