\noindent\textbf{Background and question.}\quad A PointGoal navigation (PointNav) agent is a deep-RL system that, given a goal coordinate, must walk a 3D scene to reach it. Trained agents develop \emph{cognitive-map-like} internal representations of space inside their recurrent (LSTM) memory --- an emergent phenomenon documented even in agents \emph{without} any visual input \cite{wijmans2023emergence}. The agent has two routes to the spatial information its policy needs to act each step: \textbf{(i) recompute it} from the current visual encoder output, or \textbf{(ii) maintain it across time} in the recurrent memory by integrating the GPS sensor signal. These two routes compete for finite hidden-state capacity. \emph{Does the structure of the visual input (its encoder bandwidth, spatial layout) determine how this capacity gets allocated, and therefore the format the cognitive map takes?}

\noindent\textbf{Capacity-allocation claim.}\quad A bottleneck at the encoder leaves only one route, integration, so the LSTM is forced to carry a temporally-stable, linearly-readable position code in the policy-readable layer ($\mathbf{h}_2$) --- the cognitive map. A rich encoder opens the visual-feature route; policy gradient reallocates capacity to it, and the linearly-readable component of $\mathbf{h}_2$ collapses --- though the same hidden state still encodes position, only non-linearly. \emph{The same agent achieves the same task; what differs is the format of its cognitive map.}

\noindent\textbf{Five conditions on one bandwidth axis.}\quad All agents share a $3$-layer LSTM ($512$-d) and the standard non-visual sensor stack (goal-in-start-frame, GPS, compass, close-to-goal scalar) of \cite{wijmans2023emergence}; only the visual input changes. We retrained all four sighted conditions from scratch under unified hyperparameters ($250$M frames each) to fix the inconsistency in our \emph{Report~1} runs. All achieve $96$--$99\%$ task success and SPL $\in [0.85, 0.89]$, ruling out a skill-gap explanation for any cross-condition representational difference.

\noindent\textbf{Method.}\quad For each condition we collect $500$ deterministic-policy episodes and run six probes on the top-layer hidden state $\mathbf{h}_2$. A \emph{probe} is a small regression model trained to decode some target (e.g.\ the agent's GPS coordinates) from $\mathbf{h}_2$, with $5$-fold episode-level cross-validation; high $R^2$ means the target is decodable from that representation. \emph{Linear} probes (Ridge $\alpha{=}10$) tell us what a linear policy head --- the operation DD-PPO actually performs --- could read; \emph{non-linear} ($2$-layer MLP) probes tell us what is in the representation \emph{at all} (linear-readable or not). We also use lag-$k$ probes (decoding past position from current $\mathbf{h}_t$), per-(unit, scene) Skaggs spatial information from the place-cell literature, cross-training probes (re-running the linear probe at $5$ checkpoints across training), and cross-condition CKA / Procrustes shape distance / memory transplant.

\noindent\textbf{Result --- because.}\quad Linear GPS $R^2$ falls monotonically with encoder spatial output (Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc}, solid line) --- a robust trend across the four converged sighted conditions plus the independently-retrained blind. The trend is what made foveated-logpolar (a $2{\times}2$-output rich-encoder control we added \emph{after} Report~1) a \emph{falsification target}: the encoder-bandwidth account predicts it lands in the rich-encoder regime, and Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc} shows it does --- ruling out the alternative that foveation \emph{specifically} (rather than encoder bandwidth in general) is what shapes the cognitive map. The other obvious alternative --- that the linear probe simply lacks capacity in rich-encoder hidden states --- is ruled out by distance-to-goal, a same-dimensional control target which all five conditions decode at $R^2 > 0.8$.

\noindent\textbf{Result --- but.}\quad A non-linear MLP probe on the \emph{same} hidden states recovers GPS in every condition (Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc}, dashed). The gap from linear to MLP \emph{widens with encoder bandwidth}. Position information is not destroyed by a rich encoder; it gets pushed into non-linear $\mathbf{h}_2$ directions the linear DD-PPO action head cannot reach.

\noindent\textbf{Result --- therefore.}\quad The bottleneck (no encoder, or $1{\times}1$) leaves only one route: integrate the GPS sensor across time and store the result in a linearly-readable axis. A rich encoder opens a per-step visual-feature route; policy gradient reallocates capacity, and the integrated code stops being the linearly-readable axis --- though the MLP recovery shows position remains encoded. \emph{Format differs; total information shifts only modestly.}

\noindent\textbf{Convergent evidence: capacity allocation is a training-time process.}\quad Re-running the linear probe at intermediate checkpoints (Figure~\ref{fig:subst}) shows all four sighted conditions begin training with a high linear top-layer GPS code; the linear axis is the LSTM's \emph{default} format. What separates conditions is what happens \emph{after}: bottleneck preserves the integrated code; rich-encoder conditions decay as the visual route consolidates. This rules out the alternative that bottleneck conditions never learned the visual route --- they did, but policy gradient kept the integration route on top because no usable visual alternative emerged.

\noindent\textbf{Convergent evidence: the bottleneck-state code is integrated, the rich-encoder state is reactive.}\quad Decoding past position from current $\mathbf{h}_t$ (Figure~\ref{fig:lagk}) shows bottleneck conditions sustain stable positive $R^2$ across the lag range while rich-encoder ones degrade rapidly. The contrast is about format, not total information: same MLP recovery (Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc}), different temporal dynamics. Per-(unit, scene) Skaggs spatial information is condition-flat at $[1.16, 1.35]$ bits across all five conditions, ruling out the null reading that bottleneck conditions just have more place cells; the bandwidth--allocation signal lives in the \emph{linear-readout axis}, not the per-unit metric.

\noindent\textbf{Deviation from the proposal: H1 reframed.}\quad The proposal cast H1 as compensatory memory specifically in the foveated agent, and \emph{Report~1} (on partially-converged checkpoints with heterogeneous hyperparameters) reported foveated as the longest-memory condition. Both are now \emph{superseded}. After unified retraining (which removed the apparent foveated specialness) and after adding foveated-logpolar as a falsification test, the data show a clean encoder-bandwidth trend (Figure~\ref{fig:cap_alloc}) under which foveated and uniform sit in the same rich-encoder regime. \emph{The mechanism is encoder bandwidth, not foveation specifically.}

\noindent\textbf{Deviation from the proposal: H3 de-scoped, methodology broadened.}\quad The proposal's H3 (epistemic gaze: a learned-gaze MLP targeting memory-weak regions) was de-scoped after the gaze decoder collapsed to a fixed point under pure navigation reward; a bounded-$\sigma$ stochastic-gaze fix is in the codebase but not pursued this iteration. Compensating, we imported a richer set of cognitive-neuroscience methods into the representational-analysis frame --- Skaggs spatial information as a place-cell signature on $\mathbf{h}_2$, an SR-style predictive-horizon probe, Procrustes shape-metric and principal-angle subspace divergence, an excursion-forgetting protocol, and a leave-one-scene-out scene-invariance test. \emph{The contribution shifts from a single epistemic-gaze deliverable to recasting classical cognitive-neuroscience metrics as a computational framework for representational analysis of deep-RL agents.}

\noindent\textbf{Problems encountered, solutions explored.}\quad \textbf{(i)~Hyperparameter drift across Report~1 conditions} (different $\text{num\_envs}$, $\text{lr}$, frame budgets) confounded cross-condition comparisons. Resolved by retraining all four sighted conditions from scratch under a unified config. \textbf{(ii)~Skaggs spatial information $\sim 100\times$ inflated} relative to the place-cell literature when computed on raw $\mathbf{h}_2$, because near-zero global means dominate the ratio in $I = \sum p(b)(\lambda_b/\bar\lambda)\log_2(\lambda_b/\bar\lambda)$. Resolved by applying ReLU rectification ($\max(\mathbf{h}_2, 0)$) before computing per-bin firing rates. \textbf{(iii)~Cross-condition memory transplant on coarse} failed at the state-dict load (incompatible RGB shape: $48{\times}48$ vs $256{\times}256$). Resolved by restricting to the $6$ sighted-only pairs sharing $256{\times}256$ input; coarse-and-blind transplants pending an architecture-side fix. \textbf{(iv)~Blind probe-collect at $50$ episodes gave catastrophically wide CV folds} (linear $-0.27\pm 1.41$). Diagnosed as sample-size noise: the same probe at $100$ episodes stabilises to $+0.79\pm 0.25$.

\noindent\textbf{Forward risks.}\quad (a)~The blind $500$-ep collection ETA ($\sim 6$h on RCP) overlaps with the parallel NeurIPS submission deadline; the $100$-ep partial NPZ provides a fall-back. (b)~Single-seed per condition bounds seed-variance error bars; multi-seed re-runs are out of compute scope this iteration. (c)~The transplant matrix is incomplete without coarse-and-blind pairs --- we cannot test the bottleneck-vs-rich-encoder asymmetry directly until the architecture-side fix lands.
