\section{Research question and headline finding}
% ==========================================================================

\textbf{Question.} A PointNav agent~\cite{wijmans2020ddppo,savva2019habitat} has two routes to the spatial information its policy needs to act: recompute it each step from the current visual encoder output, or maintain it across time inside the recurrent (LSTM) memory. These routes compete for finite hidden-state capacity. \emph{Does the structure of the visual input determine how this capacity gets allocated?}

\textbf{Headline finding.} Across five conditions that share architecture, sensor stack, training budget, and within-episode behaviour ($96\!\!-\!\!99\%$ success), the linear top-layer GPS $R^2$ falls \emph{monotonically} with encoder spatial output dimension: from blind ($\approx +0.79$, no encoder) and coarse ($+0.58$, $1{\times}1$), through foveated-logpolar ($-0.12$, $2{\times}2$), to foveated-blur ($+0.16$, $4{\times}4$) and uniform ($-1.19$, $4{\times}4$). The same hidden states recover GPS at $R^2 \in [0.46, 0.92]$ under a $2$-layer MLP probe, so the contrast is about \emph{linear} top-layer readability (the operation the DD-PPO action head performs), not information loss. \textbf{Theoretical claim:} bottleneck conditions force the LSTM to integrate the GPS sensor across time and store the result in a linearly-readable axis of $\mathbf{h}_2$; rich-encoder conditions reallocate this capacity to a per-step visual-feature route.

% ==========================================================================
\section{Five conditions on one bandwidth axis}
% ==========================================================================

All agents share a $3$-layer LSTM ($512$-d) and the Wijmans~\cite{wijmans2023emergence} non-visual sensor stack (goal-in-start-frame, GPS, compass, close-to-goal scalar); only the visual input changes. All four sighted conditions were retrained from scratch under unified hyperparameters ($\text{seed}{=}0$, $\text{num\_envs}{=}16$, $250$M frames), eliminating the hyperparameter inconsistency present in our \emph{Report 1} runs. All achieve $96$--$99\%$ success and SPL $\in [0.85, 0.89]$, so any cross-condition representational difference cannot be attributed to a skill gap.

For each condition we collect $500$ deterministic-policy episodes ($\sim 30$k--$120$k LSTM steps) and run six analytical lenses on the top-layer hidden state $\mathbf{h}_2$: linear (Ridge $\alpha{=}10$) and MLP ($256$ hidden, $L_2{=}10^{-4}$) probes for GPS; lag-$k$ probe for $k\in\{0,1,2,5,10,20,50\}$; per-(unit, scene) Skaggs spatial information (rectified $\max(\mathbf{h}_2,0)$); cross-training probes at five intermediate checkpoints; cross-condition CKA, Procrustes shape distance, and memory transplant. All probes use $5$-fold episode-level cross-validation. Single seed per condition follows the comparative-cognition modelling convention; rigour at the cross-condition level instead derives from convergence across the six lenses.

% ==========================================================================
\section{Results: a Because-But-Therefore chain}
% ==========================================================================

\textbf{Because: linear GPS $R^2$ falls monotonically with encoder bandwidth.} Fitting Ridge on $\mathbf{h}_2 \to$ GPS gives the headline cross-condition pattern (Table~\ref{tab:probe}, columns 2--3): bottleneck conditions retain the linear code (blind $+0.79$, coarse $+0.58$); rich-encoder conditions lose it (foveated $+0.16$, uniform $-1.19$). The third rich-encoder condition (foveated-logpolar, $2{\times}2$) sits at $-0.12$, closer to the rich-encoder regime than to coarse. Distance-to-goal, a same-dimensional control target, decodes at $R^2 \in [0.81, 0.96]$ in every condition, ruling out a probe-capacity-collapse reading.

\textbf{But: a non-linear (MLP) probe recovers GPS in every condition.} A $2$-layer MLP fit to the same hidden states recovers GPS at $R^2 \in [0.46, 0.92]$. The MLP-minus-linear gap widens monotonically with encoder bandwidth ($+0.13$ blind $\to$ $+0.22$ coarse $\to$ $+0.59$ foveated-logpolar $\to$ $+0.60$ foveated $\to$ $+1.65$ uniform). \emph{Position information is not destroyed by a rich encoder; it gets pushed into non-linear $\mathbf{h}_2$ directions that the DD-PPO linear action head cannot read.}

\textbf{Therefore: capacity allocation, not information loss.} The bottleneck (no encoder, or $1{\times}1$) forces the LSTM to integrate the GPS sensor across time --- it has no other route. A rich encoder offers a viable per-step visual-feature route to the same information; policy gradient reallocates capacity to the visual route, and the integrated code is no longer the linearly-readable axis. \emph{The same hidden state encodes position in both regimes; only the format differs.}

% ==========================================================================
\section{Convergent evidence}
% ==========================================================================

Three additional lenses corroborate the capacity-allocation account.

\textbf{Substitution dynamics across training.} Re-probing intermediate checkpoints ($\approx 50/100/150/200/250$M frames) shows that \emph{all four sighted conditions start at high linear GPS $R^2$ at $\sim 50$M frames} (coarse $+0.91$, foveated $+0.91$, uniform $+0.83$, foveated-logpolar $+0.92$); the linear top-layer code emerges early across the bandwidth spectrum. Bottleneck (coarse) preserves $R^2$ around $+0.5$ through training; rich-encoder conditions decay --- uniform crosses zero by $\sim 200$M and lands at $-1.79$; foveated drifts to $+0.18$; foveated-logpolar to $-0.03$. Decay rate tracks encoder informativeness about position --- this is capacity allocation as a \emph{training-time process}, not a property of the converged endpoint.

\textbf{Lag-$k$ temporal stability.} Decoding past position $\text{GPS}_{t-k}$ from current $\mathbf{h}_t$ across $k\in\{0,1,2,5,10,20\}$ separates \emph{integrated} from \emph{reactive} memory (Table~\ref{tab:probe}, column~5). Bottleneck conditions sustain stable positive $R^2$ across $k$ (blind: $+0.78\to+0.68$; coarse: $+0.58\to+0.53$); rich-encoder conditions are unstable (foveated: $+0.18\to-0.07$; uniform sub-baseline at $k{=}0$ already, crashing to $-6.53$ at $k{=}20$).

\textbf{Place-cell signature is condition-flat.} Per-(unit, scene) Skaggs spatial information (rectified $\max(\mathbf{h}_2,0)$) is essentially condition-flat: $1.20$ (coarse), $1.24$ (foveated), $1.19$ (uniform), $1.16$ (foveated-logpolar); blind $1.35$ (preliminary). $\Delta \approx 0.15$ bits, well under the within-condition spread. \emph{The bandwidth-allocation signal lives in the linear-readout axis, not the per-unit place-cell metric.}

% ==========================================================================
\section{What changed since Report 1; status; risks}
% ==========================================================================

\textbf{Reframe.} \emph{Report 1} reported foveated as having the longest temporal memory ($R^2{=}0.629$ at lag-$5$, the only condition still positive). \emph{This is overturned.} The Report 1 ranking reflected partially-converged training plus heterogeneous hyperparameters across conditions; after unified retraining, foveated's lag-$k$ is \emph{less} stable than coarse's, not more. The new mechanism (encoder bandwidth) makes the same predictions for blind/coarse/uniform/foveated and additionally predicts that foveated-logpolar (a $2{\times}2$ rich-encoder control not in Report 1) lands in the rich-encoder regime --- which it does ($-0.12$).

\textbf{Status.} Phase~A probe-collect: $4/5$ complete; blind $500$-ep run in flight on EPFL RCP ($\sim 6$h ETA, partial $100$-ep NPZ already used above). Phase~B (linear/MLP/lag-$k$/Skaggs/substitution/CKA-Procrustes-transplant) complete for the four sighted conditions; the blind row of each is queued to fire automatically once the $500$-ep NPZ lands. Pre-DDL queue: re-run lenses with converged blind, apply values to the NeurIPS submission, sanity-rebuild the paper PDF.

\textbf{Risks tracked.} (a)~The $50$-ep blind preview gave catastrophically wide CV folds (linear $-0.27\pm 1.41$); at $100$ eps the same probe stabilised at $+0.79\pm 0.25$. We expect $500$ eps to tighten std to $\sim 0.10$. (b)~A separately-trained blind on a different seed/effective-batch gives a different mean ($+0.36$); we use our own retrained checkpoint and treat the alternative as one cross-setup robustness check.
