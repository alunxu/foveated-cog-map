# Attractor and integrator networks in the brain

- **Authors**: Mikail Khona, Ila R. Fiete
- **Venue**: Nature Reviews Neuroscience
- **Year**: 2022
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s41583-022-00642-0
- **PDF mirror**: https://mcgovern.mit.edu/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/s41583-022-00642-0.pdf

## Abstract (paraphrased)
A comprehensive review of how the brain implements continuous attractors and integrator networks for navigation, working memory, error correction, and noisy cue integration -- including the head-direction ring, grid module, and place-cell remapping. Develops the unifying view that low-dimensional continuous-attractor dynamics is the canonical motif behind hippocampal-entorhinal coding.

## Why it is relevant to our paper
Bucket D (cognitive maps theory). Authoritative recent neuroscience review that frames our LSTM h_2 -- a 512-d recurrent integrator -- in the canonical attractor-network language. Useful as a single-stop biology citation when introducing why an integrator state could carry a position code at all, replacing several scattered older citations.
