# Relating transformers to models and neural representations of the hippocampal formation

- **Authors**: James C. R. Whittington, Joseph Warren, Timothy E. J. Behrens
- **Venue**: International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR) 2022
- **Year**: 2022
- **arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.04035
- **OpenReview**: https://openreview.net/forum?id=B8DVo9B1YE0

## Abstract (paraphrased)
Many deep neural network models of memory and the hippocampus have been proposed, while transformers have, separately, become enormously successful as sequence models. This paper shows that transformers, when equipped with recurrent position encodings, can be related directly to a current neuroscience model of the hippocampal formation (the Tolman-Eichenbaum Machine, TEM). The resulting "TEM-transformer" (TEM-t) replicates the spatially tuned representations of the hippocampal formation -- most notably grid and place cells -- while offering substantial performance gains over the original TEM in sample efficiency and accuracy on spatial-relational generalisation tasks.
