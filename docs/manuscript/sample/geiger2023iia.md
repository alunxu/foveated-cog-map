# Causal Abstraction for Faithful Model Interpretation

- **Authors**: Atticus Geiger, Christopher Potts, Thomas Icard
- **Venue**: arXiv (a unifying companion to the CLeaR / NeurIPS DAS line we already cite)
- **Year**: 2023-2024
- **arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.04709 (causal abstraction theory) / cf. NeurIPS 2023 "Interpretability at Scale: Identifying Causal Mechanisms in Alpaca" https://papers.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2023/file/f6a8b109d4d4fd64c75e94aaf85d9697-Paper-Conference.pdf

## Abstract (paraphrased)
Provides the theoretical framework that unifies activation patching, distributed alignment search (DAS), causal mediation, causal scrubbing, circuit analysis, concept erasure, sparse autoencoders, and activation steering as instances of *interchange interventions* on a causal abstraction. Defines interchange-intervention accuracy (IIA) as the central graded faithfulness metric.

## Why it is relevant to our paper
Bucket F (probing methodology + representational geometry). Our memory-transplant + ablation experiments are forms of interchange interventions in the causal-abstraction sense; citing the unifying theory gives our methodological framing modern weight beyond simple linear probes. Sits naturally next to `geiger2024finding` (which we already cite) and `heimersheim2024activation`.
