# Intrinsic Dimension, Persistent Homology and Generalization in Neural Networks

- **Authors**: Tolga Birdal, Aaron Lou, Leonidas J. Guibas, Umut Şimşekli
- **Venue**: Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS) 2021
- **Year**: 2021
- **arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2111.13171
- **OpenReview**: https://openreview.net/forum?id=099uYP0EKsJ
- **Code**: https://github.com/tolgabirdal/PHDimGeneralization

## Abstract (paraphrased)
Replaces the classical "number of parameters" complexity measure for neural networks with a fractal/persistent-homology intrinsic dimension. Demonstrates a strong empirical correlation between this PH-dimension and generalisation, supplying a more robust ID estimator than TwoNN for high-ambient-dimension representations.

## Why it is relevant to our paper
Bucket F (probing methodology + representational geometry). We use TwoNN intrinsic dimension to characterise h_2 across sensors. Birdal et al. is the canonical follow-up showing TwoNN can be noisy in high ambient dimension and proposing the persistent-homology alternative -- exactly the methodological caveat reviewers will press us on.
