# Constructing future behavior in the hippocampal formation through composition and replay

- **Authors**: James C. R. Whittington, et al. (Behrens lab)
- **Venue**: Nature Neuroscience
- **Year**: 2025
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s41593-025-01908-3
- **PMC**: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12081289/

## Abstract (paraphrased)
A successor to the Tolman-Eichenbaum Machine. Argues that hippocampal state spaces are built compositionally from primitive building blocks, so hippocampal codes can be read as compositional memories. The model predicts a specific role for replay events from newly discovered landmarks, which the authors verify in rodent recordings: replay induces and strengthens new remote firing fields.

## Why it is relevant to our paper
Bucket D (cognitive maps / hippocampus theory, recent). Our paper observes that h_2 carries a *task-functional* spatial code rather than a generic Cartesian one; this is consistent with the compositional view that the hippocampus stores task-relevant primitives, not pixel-perfect maps. Strong neuroscience anchor for our "format depends on what you compose with the sensor" framing. Replaces a more dated Behrens et al. citation.
