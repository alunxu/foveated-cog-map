# Belief inference for hierarchical hidden states in spatial navigation

- **Authors**: Risa Katayama, Ryo Shiraki, Shin Ishii, Wako Yoshida
- **Venue**: Communications Biology (Nature)
- **Year**: 2024
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s42003-024-06316-0
- **URL**: https://www.nature.com/articles/s42003-024-06316-0

## Abstract (paraphrased)
The authors develop a Bayesian hierarchical inference model of how humans solve a "Tiger maze" navigation task that requires simultaneously inferring a local hidden state and a global hidden state under probabilistic observations. fMRI shows the medial prefrontal cortex and basal ganglia engage at different layers of the inference hierarchy, depending on the level and the type of inference required.

## Why it is relevant to our paper
Bucket D (cognitive maps / hidden-state inference, recent). Complements the Sanders et al. 2020 hidden-state inference frame we already cite, but with explicit *spatial* navigation and a hierarchical structure. Useful for our consumption-axis framing: humans, like our agents, do not simply read off (x,y) but flexibly consume the integrated state for behaviour.
