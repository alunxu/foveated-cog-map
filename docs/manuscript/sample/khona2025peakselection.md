# Global modules robustly emerge from local interactions and smooth gradients

- **Authors**: Mikail Khona, Sarthak Chandra, Ila R. Fiete
- **Venue**: Nature
- **Year**: 2025 (volume 640)
- **DOI**: 10.1038/s41586-024-08541-3
- **URL**: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-08541-3

## Abstract (paraphrased)
Proposes "peak selection" as a simple mathematical principle: when a smooth gradient is paired with locally competitive interactions, modular structure emerges spontaneously. The authors apply the principle to grid cells and demonstrate that biologically plausible local lateral interactions produce discrete grid-cell modules with discretely spaced spatial periods, without external organising fields, genetic blueprints, or pre-specified modular boundaries.

## Why it is relevant to our paper
Bucket D (cognitive maps theory, recent Nature). Our paper argues that the *format* of the spatial code is shaped by the upstream sensor; Khona et al. show that grid-cell modular structure itself is shaped by *local lateral interactions*, not by the path-integration objective. Pairs naturally with Schaeffer 2022 (no-free-lunch) as evidence that emergent spatial coding requires structural constraints, not just task pressure.
